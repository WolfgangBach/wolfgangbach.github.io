Victoria Falls

Backpacking throw southern Africa by my own

Posted by Wolfgang on Friday, July 7, 2023

Leaving South Africa (02.06)

We woke up early and headed to the airport to catch a flight from Johannesburg to Victoria Falls Town. It was the smallest plane I had ever flown on for a city transfer. The airplane had just three seats in one row, with one seat on the left and two on the right side. We landed in Victoria Falls Town, Zimbabwe. Juan accompanied me to the border with Zambia since I had booked a bed in a hostel in Livingstone. As we walked over the bridge that goes over a cliff, we could see the water flowing from Victoria Falls. We said goodbye, and I continued my journey to Zambia/Livingstone. At the hostel in Livingstone, I met two girls who had studied in London for the past few years. Katie was originally from the UK, and Rozalia was originally from Poland but had also lived in Switzerland for a long time.

Trip to Victoria Falls (03.06)

The hostel offered a free shuttle to Victoria Falls on the Zambian side. I decided to visit the falls and was joined by the two girls and another guy from Israel. The four of us spent a lovely day exploring Victoria Falls. It was incredible to see the waterfall. After a long day of sightseeing, the Israeli guy and I returned to the hotel to relax. I needed to research and plan my master’s work and figure out how to travel to Namibia from Livingstone.

A Relaxing Day (04.06)

There wasn’t much to talk about this day. I sorted some pictures, chilled out a bit, and looked at my plans for the upcoming weeks. That’s all I remember. ;)

A day tour to Botswana (05.06)

The Katie and Rozalia from the hostel had booked a water land tour in Botswana, and I decided to join them on the same tour. I was hoping to see hippos up close from the boat. The tour started at the hostel, where a bus picked up all the clients. We drove to the Botswana border, where we went through immigration. Then we boarded a safari jeep witch drove us to the river between Namibia and Botswana. Where food was prepared for us, but there was an issue with my safari ticket that almost prevented me from joining the safari tour. Thankfully, I resolved the problem, and I hopped on the boat where everyone was already waiting. As we cruised down the river, we saw many animals on the riverbank and in the water. The hippos were the most impressive creatures, especially since they were so close to the boat. It was truly remarkable! After the boat tour, we also had a driving tour through the national park next to the river. While it didn’t have as many animals as Kruger, it was still enjoyable to see the animals on the beach. In the end, we even spotted three female lions. We were then brought back to the border, and a bus took us back to the hostel.

7 Hours for 200 km (06.06)

The bus tour to Namibia began at 5 in the morning, and the bus seemed to be in decent condition. Rozalia and Katie had booked the same bus and wie were all heading to Katima Mulilo in Namibia and also taking the same flight on the next day to Windhoek. We found our seats, but I had to share mine with a guy who had his small bag next to him so he also was occupying part of my space. At the front of the bus, there were even two people without seats, and TVs and other items were packed in the walkway between the seats. After a while, the road became very rough. The bus driver drove mostly beside the road to avoid the potholes, causing us to travel at a slow pace. We passed villages where it seemed that people didn’t have access to electricity or modern infrastructure. The houses were small and made of clay, wood, and hay.

After a very long bus ride of around 7 hours, we reached the Zambian-Namibian border. We crossed the border on foot and went into the Namibian office to immigrate. Katie and Rozalia went first in and I looked at our stuff. Rozalia came out with a shocked and angry face, she could not get into Namibia with her polish passport. People from Poland and other easter european countries needed to apply for a visa before entering. Luckily, that she also had a swiss passport and where allowed to enter the country with that. After that I was also very scared of not getting into the country, but with a German passport it was not a problem. Wee all got throw immigration and entered Namibia, arriving in the city of Katima Mulilo. There, we were picked up by the accommodation we had booked for the night. The pickup vehicle had only two seats in the front, so one of the girls and I had to sit in the back area without seats. It was interesting to note that this was allowed in Namibia. We had a good evening at the bar in the accommodation and then went to bed to be fit for the further travel.
